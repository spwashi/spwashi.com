Local Proof Cards

Screenshot-native records for asks, offers, pledges, references, and decisions.

A Local Proof Card is a small, readable record you can keep. It can describe what someone needs, what someone offers, what a group agreed to, what a project is asking for, or what insight should be remembered.

The card stays useful as a screenshot, a copied seed, a link, or a later archived record. No wallet required. No platform lock-in required. The first version is local, readable, and shareable.

?definition

What A Card Does

A card makes a claim. A screenshot preserves it. A URL or seed lets someone revisit it. A later version can supersede it.

These are NFT-like in the narrow sense that each card is a distinct object with its own context. They are not speculative assets. They are local records first.

?safety

Keep The Record Honest

A card is not proof that a claim is true. It is proof that a claim was made in a readable form at a specific moment.

If a card changes, make a new version. Do not silently overwrite the record.

#>consensus_flow

Screenshot To Consensus

Consensus does not mean everyone agrees forever. It means the group has a visible reference point for what is being proposed, supported, questioned, blocked, revised, or archived.

Draft

A proposal exists. No agreement yet.

Supported

Enough people support moving forward.

Concerned

People agree partly but have notes.

Blocked

Someone names a concrete reason not to proceed.

Revised

A new version replaces the old one.

Archived

No longer active, but preserved.

Screenshot the card before the conversation spreads out. The card gives the group one thing to point at.
