RPG Wednesday

A recurring Wednesday tabletop RPG session and a networking surface for collaboration under enchantment. The question is not just what happens in a fantastical scenario, but how someone navigates it with fictional tools toward shared understanding. Start with a character sheet, then move into the session log, world, and cast once the canon has enough weight to keep.

Character sheets are the right first artifact. Sessions are dated events. The world and cast become persistent references once details recur enough to matter. The local gameplay kit stores private table state in this browser for use during play.

Part of the wager here is that software engineering and entertainment are already compromising around the interactive appeal of games toward productive and sustainable ecosystems with different scopes of immersion. I want this surface to describe a medium that can develop over time and clarify what principal engineers might consider.

@local_gameplay_kit

Local Gameplay Kit

Enable JavaScript to use private localStorage tools for character-sheet drafting, scene state, initiative, clocks, notes, a table dashboard, and copyable session-log seeds. If you want the public-facing version first, begin with the character-sheet builder; the campaign pages remain readable without any scripting.

Code can be read out loud on TikTok. A group chat lets multiple creators share a rhythm of production, maintain momentum, and keep one person from burning out while the canon develops.

@asset_capture_frames

Asset Capture Frames

Planned next: a private browser-local asset register for player-provided images. The site stages the image and text; the player captures the card, shares it into TikTok or Discord, and turns the screenshot into prompt material once the group has agreed on the canon.

4:5 Character portrait Name, role, faction, palette, mood, and sheet cues in a clean vertical format.
1:1 Item card One subject, strong silhouette, optional tags, and a concise prompt band.
16:9 Place card Location, scene context, weather, threat level, and a wide composition.
9:16 Threat reveal A vertical format for foes, omens, clocks, and dramatic reveals.

Storage boundary

Images should live in IndexedDB. localStorage should keep only metadata such as ids, labels, tags, character-sheet fields, selected motif, and active capture preset.

Sticker prompts

Once a TikTok group has a streak, stickers and screenshots become prompt material for Gemini or Midjourney. Saving stickers creates a reusable collection instead of a one-off image dump, and the group chat keeps the production beat distributed across multiple creators.

Screenshot mode

Controls recede, contrast increases, the frame locks to an aspect ratio, and an exit path remains visible. The screenshot stays manual.

Canon channels

Discord keeps the live memory. The #rpg-beat channel carries character art and scene inspiration between sessions so group consciousness can maintain the canon. Streaks, stickers, and callbacks become a QA loop this surface should eventually make easy for other teams to copy.

I am currently fundraising and storyboarding for an animated series with Raven of the Broken Biscuits. Later this surface should have a credited place for Raven's art that does not imply it is mine.

The character

'l'n'd'r Bjrnkptpf is the main campaign character: a rock gnome made from clay of our universe, earth of genre gathered into one body so production attention has somewhere to go. Bluish-grey skin like modeling clay, gemdust freckles, and speech bubble physics treated as an actual material problem. The character exists to study the geology and enchantment required for collaborative language to become visible.

Gnomes were the familiar starting point, not the limit. The goal is depth without caricature: begin with a lineage that can hold craft, then explore stranger species and traditions as the canon grows. Words are still the spell. Boon materializes as a nucleation field, Bone as the lattice that carries structure, and HONK remains pure propagation.
