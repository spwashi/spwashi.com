RPG Wednesday

A recurring Wednesday tabletop RPG session. This page starts with the table tools, then points to the session log, world, and cast.

Sessions are dated events. The world and cast are persistent references that become useful after details recur. The local gameplay kit stores private table state in this browser for use during play.

#>rpg_wednesday
#:layer #!play
#:surface #!event_log

^"structure"{
 sessions: ~"./sessions/"   @event  session log, dated /YYYY-MM-DD/
 world:    ~"./world/"      ^object  stable setting and lore
 cast:     ~"./cast/"       ~refs    character reference nodes
 arcs:     ~"./arcs/"       ?[probe  narrative thread groupings
}

Sessions are events at specific moments in time. The world is the stable setting those events happen inside. Cast members are references back into sessions. Arcs are open questions carried across multiple sessions.

The URL structure follows the Spw operator model. See the grammar surface and the deeper spw-workbench notes.

play surface rpg wednesday @ sessions ^ world ~ cast ?[ arcs @ local kit @ capture frames
@local_gameplay_kit

Local Gameplay Kit

Enable JavaScript to use private localStorage tools for scene state, initiative, clocks, notes, a table dashboard, and copyable session-log seeds. The campaign pages remain readable without it.

@asset_capture_frames

Asset Capture Frames

Planned next: a private browser-local asset register for player-provided images. The site stages the image and text; the player captures the card and decides what to do with it outside the site.

4:5 Character portrait Name, role, faction, palette, and mood in a clean vertical format.
1:1 Item card One subject, strong silhouette, optional tags, and a concise prompt band.
16:9 Place card Location, scene context, weather, threat level, and a wide composition.
9:16 Threat reveal A vertical format for foes, omens, clocks, and dramatic reveals.

Storage boundary

Images should live in IndexedDB. localStorage should keep only metadata such as ids, labels, tags, selected motif, and active capture preset.

Prompt companion

Prompt text should describe subject, role, mood, palette, scene context, style constraints, and negative notes without assuming a specific external service.

Screenshot mode

Controls recede, contrast increases, the frame locks to an aspect ratio, and an exit path remains visible. The screenshot stays manual.

Exploration motifs

Operator palettes, land-cluster tones, constellation marks, archive stamps, and brace treatments can become optional local presentation rewards.

'l'n'd'r

The character

'l'n'd'r is a gnome who does speech bubble magic. Glowing blue eyes, ornate hat, magnificent beard — the kind of presence that makes vocabulary float visibly in the air. Words are the spell: Boon materializes as a nucleation field, Bane as an activation threshold, Bone as the lattice that carries structure, HONK as pure propagation. Language is not metaphor here; it is the medium of effect.

'l'n'd'r represents linguistic wonder — the practice of treating vocabulary as a material that can be shaped, concentrated, fermented, and emitted. The vocabulary garden image was generated during worldbuilding; the gnome appeared at center unprompted. That's the character.
