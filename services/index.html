Services

I'm Spwashi. I build software and make art. This page is how I find work, how I pass along work I can't do alone, and how I let new collaborators find the right door.

There are three registers here, and they serve different people. Each leads to a resting point where you can think without commitment, then follow a link into the next most useful page.

  • What I build directly — cover design, illustration, ebook formatting, web systems, consulting, QA. Split across creator work and systems work, with the tools page for reusable self-description and planning.
  • Who I can connect you with — an ecosystem of authors, editors, engineers, entrepreneurs, and makers. Referrals without losing the thread, and a useful ramp for someone still early in their career path.
  • How I keep the work honest — care practices that precede and accompany the commercial work: journaling, community, movement, reading. Therapy if those aren't enough, plus the mental health topic when you want the broader context first.

Bookmark a resting point for a friend who needs a tangible step forward. Sharing a page is lower stakes than recommending a person — it lets someone arrive on their own time.

Three Paths

Each panel is a short summary and a link. No page tries to do everything on its own.

Creator Work

For authors, illustrators, and independent makers. Cover design, illustration, interactive book covers, ebook formatting. If you want editing or a co-writer alongside a genre, I likely know the right person.

Bookmark this for an author friend, a visual collaborator, or a developer who needs a humane entry point into publishing work.

Systems Work

For businesses and founders. Web development, custom applications, consulting, staff-level advisory, and QA with a team of thirty. Custom and hybrid quotes on request, especially when the work needs to outlast a single launch cycle.

Bookmark this for an owner, operator, or technical lead who wants a calm second opinion on the road ahead.

Ecosystem

Engineers, entrepreneurs, authors, editors, illustrators, librarians, gardeners. If you want to join a group or want me to pass a project along, this is the place. It also helps a student or early-career visitor see that careers are often adjacent before they are linear.

Referrals shouldn't end the thought — the page stays after the introduction, with links into the work that follows.

Care Practices

Software engineering can be isolating. Before therapy, or alongside it, try journaling, reading, movement, shared art time, and community work. If you want the public-statistics and identity context first, start with the mental health topic. If those routes still are not enough, the care intake form helps you arrive at a first conversation already knowing what to say.

For anyone wondering what to try next.

Configure Your Engagement

Four quiet dimensions — time, scope, depth, tenure — resolve into a clear tier. The center commits; everything else is a probe.

How the field works

Time pushes toward Creator Packages. Tenure often stabilizes into Business Web. Depth pulls toward Staff-Level Consulting. Scope amplifies every direction. The center resolves when one tier clearly leads. Below that threshold the right path is a conversation, not a formula — and the custom quote panel exists for exactly that.

~"custom_quote"

Custom & Hybrid Quotes

Most real work doesn't fit a pre-priced box. If your project blends a creator need with a systems need, or sits between two tiers, say so.

Send a rough sketch — a paragraph is plenty. Include what you're trying to make, what you've tried, and any constraint that matters (budget, deadline, team). I'll reply with an honest scope and a number or range within a few release cycles. If the project is a fit, I usually prefer payment up front or at the start of a phase; that keeps upbeat coordination from turning into unpaid project management.

@ start a custom quote @ email directly

Expectation: honest scoping over aggressive selling. Upfront payment is often the cleanest way to cover coordination, preparation, and the psychological load of keeping momentum visible. If I'm not the right person, I'll say so and pass you to someone in the ecosystem.

*"cadence"

Release Cadence

Release days are the 13th and 26th of each month. Twenty-four releases a year — one step more than nothing.

If you're starting a project and don't know how to pace it, you're welcome to borrow the rhythm. Two intentional steps a month is modest and cumulative. I ship on those days whether or not anyone is watching, which means contracts anchored to them have real landmarks.
