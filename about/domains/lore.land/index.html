lore.land

lore.land is the long-form storytelling surface in the wider constellation — a years-long exploration of how story can carry shared meaning alongside the land cluster. The current practical version is simpler: it should help onboard authors from note systems into ebooks, lore development, and more intentional public narrative surfaces. RPG Wednesday now acts as one of the live proving grounds under that horizon, where characters like WAPboy, Honk Bazongas, and Gravy Davis can be pressure-tested in session form before they harden into serial canon.

Role

Long-form narrative and ebook surface, with room for serial arcs, worldbuilding, computational metaphor, and live-tested campaign figures that earn a larger reading life.

Relation

One of the clearest bridges between storytelling, systems, and shared symbolic structure. The software surface shows the same grammar from the tooling side, the newer math routes help make the abstractions gentler enough to teach, and RPG Wednesday tests whether the language can survive live play before it graduates into a longer-form reading environment. Nearby surfaces like texture.website and a future trope.wiki-style register can later help separate material language from named genre moves.

Author onboarding

I think Obsidian can be a strong private starting point for authors developing notes, links, characters, motifs, and world structure. lore.land is the public companion layer where those notes can become ebook-facing lore instead of staying trapped in a vault.

Signal Studies

These studies let lore.land carry a darker register: stone, orbit, archive, and signal sitting inside the warmer site field without asking every page to darken with them. They also help define the tone for a world where computation can be narrated as a kind of universal magic without losing its engineering spine.

Palette

The home surface stays warm and conversational. Lore.land can hold a heavier material language: darker stone, brighter signal, slower orbit. That weight helps the domain feel more like a reading environment than a loose moodboard.

If this register keeps growing, it can earn tuned variants later instead of being flattened into the baseline palette too early. The goal is a worldbuilding and ebook surface authors can actually inhabit.
