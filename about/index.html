About

I do my best work where software architecture, interaction design, information design, and clear systems meet. I am using those strengths as a bridge to expand my awareness into formal Computer Science, Linguistics, and Staff-level engineering breadth.

The current public direction investigates how those deep mechanics work: local-first software, exploring how data structures and algorithms drive renderers and schedulers, and building public pages that use interactive semantics to wonder about depth I haven't learned yet.

Systems Register

I build systems

I have spent more than a decade building frontend systems, developer tooling, content platforms, and product architecture. The current parser/runtime thread is most visible in spw-workbench.

  • Language tooling and parser-driven systems
  • Interface architecture and design systems
  • Developer experience and workflow design
  • Accessible web applications and content surfaces

Edges I'm pushing

While I optimize for stable structure and readable files, I want to expand my awareness of the deep algorithmic work underneath the tools I use every day.

  • Data structures underlying text layout renderers
  • Algorithms for compression and concurrent schedulers
  • Computational linguistics and rigorous formal grammar
  • Staff-level system architecture and API contract design

Why I am applying to Obsidian

Obsidian sits close to the problems I already care about: local-first trust, metadata-rich files, extensible tooling, and the relationship between structure and interaction.

  • Local-first architecture matches how I think about ownership and user trust.
  • Editor, plugin, LSP, and metadata-heavy systems are exactly where I like to work.
  • Small teams with strong product taste are where I can contribute most effectively.

Semantics as Spells

My interest in Spw semantics functions almost like spells—small mnemonic tools that open up the underlying mechanics of a system.

Following an operator or a syntax token often leads me to wonder about deep architectural truths I haven't fully learned yet. The structure is the start of the interaction.

Land Cluster

The land cluster is a small symbolic system. Boon and bane define the polarity, bone carries the structure, bonk marks the event, and honk sends the signal outward.

  • > boon +1 · arrival, benefit — the attractive field
  • ! bane −1 · departure, cost — the bounding constraint

Current Direction

I am aligning the public work around one durable thread: advancing the rigor of plain-text systems by learning their heavy algorithmic backing. This includes building richer parser syntax, efficient layout renderers, data compression strategies for syntax trees, and precise LSP-aware navigation. The clearest entry points into this engineering work are the software surface and spw-workbench.

That connects Spw, this site's underlying rendering logic, the Obsidian application architecture, and my interest in high-performance learning environments that keep execution, annotation, and formal structure tightly coupled.
