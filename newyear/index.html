^"vessels"

Vessels

Joy needs a vessel or it evaporates by the third day of January. A vessel is not storage — it is a relay object: it holds the feeling and survives transit to another person.

Print objects

  • Annual seed card — the minimal form
  • Pamphlet — one folded sheet, one year's commitments
  • Folio — a bundle, slightly more elaborate
  • Edition — the year as a collectible, serial object

Practice objects

  • Recipe — a culinary ritual that repeats annually
  • Study plan — a curricular commitment with checkpoints
  • Mathematical habit — one problem family to return to
  • Material experiment — one physical practice to attempt

Signal objects

  • Note — a private anchor, set and revisited
  • Spell — a dense seed with serialization bias
  • Message — joy in transit to a specific person
  • Prompt — handed to a language model for elaboration

What makes a vessel work

A vessel works when it holds its shape after the midnight feeling is gone. It should be beautiful enough to keep, dense enough to use, and structured enough to hand to someone without explanation.

The season provides the frame. The vessel provides the structure. You provide the intent.

~"bundles"

Bundles

A bundle is compression as judgment: what deserves another cycle. Not everything from last year. Not a fresh start. A considered carry.

One promise

Not a list. One load-bearing thing stated plainly. It should survive being read in March without explanation.

One scientific question

A question you are genuinely uncertain about. Something real enough to return to. The question outlasts the year if it is the right one.

One mathematical habit

A problem family, a visualization practice, a daily estimation. Something that builds intuition slowly.

One culinary ritual

A dish, a fermentation, a preparation. Something you make once and return to each year. The ritual carries memory.

New year joy as fermentation starter: compress and seal what is alive now, so it has more life when you open it in spring.

Revisit Later

Some things need more time before they resolve. Set them down here — not as bookmarks, but as temporal anchors. They should return in March with more life than they had in January.
